Aristotle's Poetics
Aristotle said it 2,300 years ago: "Every story is made of three parts — a beginning, a middle, and an end."

It sounds simple. But most people start in the middle, blur through the action, and skip the end.

Today: count all three.

Beginning
It was a Tuesday. I know because Tuesdays were always the same — coffee at 7:15, the same train, the same headphones, the same playlist.
Inciting Incident
The message arrived at 9:47, while I was in a meeting about Q3 projections. One line. My father's number.
Conflict
Two parts of me collided: the professional who couldn't leave, and the son who knew this call didn't happen on ordinary days.
Recognition
I stepped out. The corridor was empty. I realised I had been treating every Tuesday like it was guaranteed.
Reversal
Everything about that morning — the coffee, the train — had been pointing at something I refused to see.
Emotional Release
He was fine. A small scare. But I haven't listened to that playlist since.
