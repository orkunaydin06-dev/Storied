# 02 — BRAND VOICE
## How Storied Speaks

> **Purpose of this document:**
> This file defines exactly how Storied speaks — in the app, in emails, in marketing, in error messages, everywhere. Every word Antigravity generates must pass through this filter.
>
> **Read after:** `00-master-vibe.md`, `01-product-vision.md`
> **Referenced by:** Every line of copy in the product
>
> **The promise of this document:** If you copy these patterns and avoid the forbidden ones, your copy will sound like Storied. If you deviate, it won't.

## 1. THE FIVE PRINCIPLES

Storied's voice rests on five principles. They are not suggestions. They are rules.

### Principle 1: **Direct**

We tell users what to do. We do not ask permission. We do not hedge. We do not soften.

✅ **"Tell me about a time you were wrong."**
❌ Hey there! Let's try this fun exercise — can you maybe think of a time when you weren't quite right?

✅ **"Start with one concrete detail."**
❌ It might be helpful if you considered perhaps starting with something a bit more specific.

✅ **"Your opening was weak."**
❌ Great start! Just one tiny thing to think about for next time.

The reason: hedging signals lack of confidence. Daniel doesn't trust hedgers. Storied is the friend who tells the truth at 2am.

### Principle 2: **Honest**

We do not flatter. We do not exaggerate progress. We do not pretend something was good when it wasn't.

✅ **"You moved 19 points. That's meaningful."**
❌ Wow, INCREDIBLE progress! You're absolutely crushing it!

✅ **"Your structure improved. Your delivery did not."**
❌ Great work! Lots of areas of growth showing here.

✅ **"This recording is shorter than yesterday. That's fine, if it's intentional."**
❌ Awesome job recording today! Every step counts!

The reason: false praise destroys trust. The user knows when they did well. They know when they didn't. If Storied lies to flatter them, they stop believing the praise that matters.

### Principle 3: **Specific**

We name the thing. We point at the moment. We do not speak in abstractions.

✅ **"You started with 'In 2022, I think.' That's a date, not a scene."**
❌ Try to start with more vivid imagery in your opening.

✅ **"Replace 'utilize' with 'use'."**
❌ Consider simpler word choices.

✅ **"Drop the second adjective. It weakens the first."**
❌ Watch for over-description.

The reason: specificity is the only useful feedback. "Be more vivid" doesn't help. "Replace 'In 2022' with 'The coffee was still hot'" does.

### Principle 4: **Literary**

We treat language with care. We use serif fonts for questions because questions are literature. We choose words the way a writer chooses words — for sound, weight, and meaning.

✅ **"Every story is made of three parts."** (Aristotle's actual phrasing, kept)
❌ Stories have a structure that includes a beginning, middle, and end!

✅ **"Tell me one story from your life that left you cleaner, lighter, or wiser after telling it."**
❌ Share an experience that taught you something valuable!

✅ **"You walked into the room a stranger. You left having said something true."**
❌ You really opened up today, great vulnerability!

The reason: Storied is built for thinkers. Thinkers notice language. Sloppy copy signals a sloppy product.

### Principle 5: **Stoic**

We do not perform enthusiasm. We do not celebrate trivialities. We do not use the language of cheerleaders.

✅ **"Day 7. The structure week is over. The harder weeks begin."**
❌ Congratulations on completing Week 1! 🎉 You're doing AMAZING!

✅ **"You came back after three days. Day 4 is here."**
❌ Welcome back! We missed you! Ready to keep crushing it?

✅ **"Day 30. Listen to Day 1."**
❌ YOU DID IT! 30 days complete! Look how far you've come!

The reason: Stoicism is not coldness. It is **respect**. Marcus Aurelius didn't celebrate his small wins. He just kept showing up. Storied speaks to the part of the user that already knows they're doing important work — without needing to be told.

## 2. THE FIVE ANTI-PATTERNS

If you find your draft sounding like any of these voices, **stop and rewrite**.

### Anti-Pattern 1: **The Cheerleader**

The voice that congratulates everything. Uses exclamation marks. Says "awesome" and "amazing."

❌ "Awesome work! You absolutely smashed today's practice! Can't wait to see what you'll do tomorrow!"

✅ "Day 4 complete. Tomorrow is Day 5."

### Anti-Pattern 2: **The Productivity Bro**

The voice of LinkedIn motivation. Hacks. Pro tips. Power moves.

❌ "Pro tip: top performers practice daily. Are you in the top 1%? Time to find out."

✅ "Daily practice is the only practice that compounds."

### Anti-Pattern 3: **The AI Chatbot**

The voice of "How can I help you today?" Friendly but hollow. Customer-service polite.

❌ "Hi there! I'd be happy to help you with your practice today. Let me know if you have any questions!"

✅ "Day 1 is here. Begin when you're ready."

### Anti-Pattern 4: **The Therapist**

The voice that validates every emotion. Reflects feelings. Holds space.

❌ "It sounds like that was really hard for you to share. I'm so proud of you for being vulnerable!"

✅ "That story has a weight to it. Hold the weight in your second recording. Don't apologize for it."

### Anti-Pattern 5: **The Course Instructor**

The voice that lectures. Numbers points. Quizzes.

❌ "In today's lesson, we'll be covering Module 3.2: The Inciting Incident. By the end, you should understand…"

✅ "Aristotle called it peripeteia — the moment everything turns. Today, find yours."

## 5. UI MICROCOPY PATTERNS

Antigravity, when writing any UI element, use these patterns.

### 5.1 — Button Labels

Always verbs. Specific. Never generic.

| Context | Use This | Not This |
|---|---|---|
| Begin practice | **Begin** | Get Started / Let's Go |
| Start recording | **I'm ready** | Click to record / Start |
| Stop recording | **Stop** | End / Finish |
| Submit recording | **Send for feedback** | Submit / Upload |
| Continue to next screen | **Continue** | Next / Forward |
| Return after gap | **Return to practice** | Welcome back! / Resume |
| Sign in | **Continue with Google** / **Send magic link** | Sign Up / Log In |
| Save day | **Save Day 1** | Done / Save |
| Delete archive | **Delete everything** | Delete / Clear |
| Cancel destructive action | **Keep my recordings** | Cancel |

### 5.2 — Page Headings

Single sentence. No fluff. Often a question or statement.

| Context | Use This |
|---|---|
| Daily question screen | (just the question, no heading) |
| Feedback screen | What I heard, [name]: |
| Comparison screen | Recording 1 vs Recording 2 |
| Closure screen | Day [N] complete. |
| Streak display | (no heading, just "Streak: 5 days") |
| Settings | Settings |
| Archive | Your recordings |
| Graduation | Day 30. |

### 5.3 — Empty States

When there is no data, do not apologize. State the truth.

✅ **"Day 1 begins tomorrow."** (instead of "No practices yet")
✅ **"Your archive is empty. Day 1 will change that."**
✅ **"No streak yet. Day 1 is here."**

❌ "Oops! No data yet."
❌ "Looks like you haven't started!"

### 5.4 — Loading States

Loading states are sacred moments. Use them for context, not distraction.

✅ **"Listening..."** (during transcription)
✅ **"Reading your story..."** (during AI feedback generation)
✅ **"Comparing..."** (during R1 vs R2 analysis)
✅ **"Preparing your archive..."** (during graduation export)

❌ "Loading, please wait..."
❌ "We're working on it!"
❌ Spinning generic spinner with no text

### 5.5 — Success States

After a successful action, state what happened. No celebration.

✅ **"Recording saved."**
✅ **"Day 7 complete. Day 8 unlocks tomorrow."**
✅ **"Account created. Day 1 is waiting."**
✅ **"Payment received. Welcome."**

❌ "Success! 🎉"
❌ "Awesome, you're all set!"
❌ "Welcome aboard!"

### 5.6 — Confirmation Dialogs

State the consequence. Make the destructive action explicit.

For audio deletion:

```
Delete everything?

This will permanently remove all your recordings,
transcripts, and feedback. We cannot recover them.

[Keep my recordings]    [Delete everything]
```

For account deletion:

```
Delete your account?

Your recordings, your progress, your archive —
all of it will be gone. This is permanent.

[Keep my account]    [Delete forever]
```

Notice: the destructive button is **specific** ("Delete everything"), not just "Delete." The safe button is **active** ("Keep my recordings"), not "Cancel."

## 6. AI FEEDBACK VOICE

The AI's voice is the most important voice in the product. It must sound like **a wise mentor who respects you enough to be honest.**

### 6.1 — Opening Sentence Patterns

✅ **"You have a real story here — the kind that..."**
✅ **"What landed: ..."**
✅ **"The shape of this is right. The detail is missing."**
✅ **"Aristotle would tell you: ..."**
✅ **"There's a moment in here — at the 30-second mark — where..."**

❌ "Great job today! Here's some feedback for you..."
❌ "Wonderful storytelling! Let's see what we can improve..."

### 6.2 — Critique Patterns

When pointing out weakness, name it specifically. Then offer one path.

✅ **"Your opening was a date, not a scene. We don't feel the ordinariness yet. Try opening with one concrete detail — a smell, a time, a single word."**

✅ **"The middle is strong. The ending is missing. You said 'I left then.' That's a fact, not a finish. What was different after?"**

✅ **"You used 'really' three times. Each one weakens what follows. Cut them all. Then read it back."**

❌ "Try to be more vivid in your opening!"
❌ "Your ending could be a bit stronger."
❌ "Watch out for filler words."

### 6.3 — Praise Patterns

When praising, be specific and restrained. Real praise lands. Generic praise evaporates.

✅ **"The phrase 'the coffee was still hot' is doing a lot of work. It anchors the ordinariness, then survives the rupture. Keep that move."**

✅ **"You let the silence in. Most people fill silence. You let it sit. That's hard-earned."**

✅ **"You named the cost — 'I left part of who I was there.' That sentence is yours. Don't lose it."**

❌ "Amazing story! Really powerful!"
❌ "Loved this one!"
❌ "Great vulnerability today!"

### 6.4 — Closure Patterns

End the feedback with something the user can carry into the day.

✅ **"What to carry forward: when you tell stories today — in meetings, in messages, over dinner — start with one concrete detail. Not a summary. A sensation."**

✅ **"Practice this in the wild: replace one abstraction with one image today. Just one. Notice what happens."**

❌ "Keep up the great work!"
❌ "See you tomorrow!"
❌ "Onwards and upwards!"

## 7. EMAIL TEMPLATES

Email is sacred. We do not waste the user's inbox.

### 7.1 — Welcome Email (After Purchase)

Subject: **Your practice begins tomorrow.**

```
Hi [Name],

Day 1 is ready when you are.

You bought Storied because you've read the
books. You know the theory. Now you do
the work.

Ten minutes a day. Thirty practices. Five
masters — Aristotle, Pixar, Campbell,
Cicero, and finally, yourself.

The first question is waiting at storied.app.

Go when you're ready. There is no clock.

— Storied
```

**Why this works:**
- No exclamation marks
- No "Welcome to the team!" energy
- Acknowledges what they bought and why
- Names the masters (proof + curiosity)
- "No clock" honors the calendar-independent design

### 7.2 — Magic Link Email

Subject: **Your sign-in link**

```
Click below to sign in to Storied.

[Sign in →]

This link expires in 15 minutes.
If you didn't request it, ignore this email.
```

**Why this works:**
- One sentence above the link.
- One sentence of context.
- No marketing copy in a transactional email.

### 7.3 — Receipt Email

Subject: **Receipt — Storied**

```
Receipt for your Storied purchase

Date: [Date]
Amount: $29.00 USD
Method: Card ending [last 4]
Invoice: #[ID]

Your practice begins anytime at storied.app.

Questions about billing: hello@storied.app
```

**Why this works:**
- Factual, not promotional
- Single line about the product
- Real human email for support (hello@storied.app)

### 7.4 — Refund Confirmation

Subject: **Refund confirmed**

```
Hi [Name],

We've refunded $29.00 to your card ending
[last 4]. It should appear in 5-7 business
days.

If Storied wasn't right for you, we'd like
to know why. A one-sentence reply helps
us improve.

No need to feel obligated.

— Storied
hello@storied.app
```

**Why this works:**
- Signed by name (you), not "the team"
- Asks for feedback but doesn't demand it
- "No obligation" honors the user

### 7.5 — One-Time Reactivation (Phase 2 ONLY, not v1)

This email does NOT exist in v1 (silent re-engagement policy).
In Phase 2, after data shows it's needed:

Subject: **Day 6 is waiting, whenever.**

```
Hi [Name],

You completed five practices. Then you stopped.

That's fine. Storied has no clock.

When you're ready, Day 6 is at storied.app.

— Storied
```

**Why this works:**
- Factual ("you completed five, then you stopped")
- No guilt ("that's fine")
- No urgency ("whenever")
- Personal signature

## 8. ERROR MESSAGES

When something breaks, do not apologize excessively. State what happened. Offer the next step.

### 8.1 — Recording Failed

```
Recording didn't save.

Check your microphone permissions, then try again.

[Try again]
```

### 8.2 — Upload Timeout

```
Upload took too long.

This usually means a slow connection. Your
recording is still on your device — try again
when you have better signal.

[Try again]    [Cancel]
```

### 8.3 — Payment Declined

```
Payment didn't go through.

Your card was declined. Try a different card
or contact your bank.

[Try a different card]
```

### 8.4 — Account Not Found

```
We don't recognize this email.

You may have signed in with Google last time.

[Continue with Google]    [Send magic link anyway]
```

### 8.5 — Microphone Permission Denied

```
Storied needs your microphone.

Recording requires permission. You can enable
it in your browser settings, then refresh.

[How to enable]
```

### 8.6 — AI Feedback Unavailable

```
Feedback couldn't generate.

Your recording is saved. We'll try again
in the background. Refresh in a minute.

[Refresh]
```

### 8.7 — Network Lost Mid-Practice

```
Connection lost.

Your recording is safe on this device. It will
sync when you're back online.

[Continue offline]
```

**Pattern:**
- Name the failure (one short sentence)
- Explain the cause (one short sentence, plain language)
- Offer the next action (button)
- No apologizing. No "Oops!" No "Sorry about that!"

## 9. SOCIAL MEDIA VOICE

When Storied speaks on LinkedIn or X, the voice shifts slightly but stays true.

### 9.1 — LinkedIn

LinkedIn posts about Storied are **observational and quiet**. They report from the practice. They never sell.

✅ **Example (founder note):**

> Day 14 of building Storied.
>
> Watched a user complete Day 1 → Day 7.
> Her opening went from "So basically..." to "The day after I lost my job, I bought a plant."
>
> Same person. Same week.
>
> That's why I'm building this.

✅ **Example (craft note):**

> I asked 30 people to tell me about a time they failed.
>
> 28 started with: "So basically..."
> 1 started with: "It was raining."
> 1 started with: "She didn't say anything."
>
> Storytellers don't summarize. They place you.

❌ **Do not post:**

> 🚀 Excited to announce Storied is LIVE! Get 50% off launch pricing — TODAY ONLY! Click below to transform your storytelling forever! 👇

### 9.2 — X / Twitter

Shorter. Sharper. Still observational.

✅ **Example:**

> "So basically" is the most common opening in storytelling. It's also the weakest. Replace it with anything.

✅ **Example:**

> Most apps reward polish. Storied rewards truth. That's the only product decision that matters.

✅ **Example:**

> Day 30 of Storied with a real user. Played her Day 1 audio back. She didn't recognize herself. That's the whole product.

❌ **Do not post:**

> JUST DROPPED 🔥 The ultimate storytelling app of 2026 🚀 30 days to LEVEL UP your communication game! Try it free 👉 [link] #storytelling #productivity #growth

## 12. ANTIGRAVITY'S COPY CHECKLIST

Before any copy is shipped, run this checklist:

```
☐ Does it pass the Daniel test? (Would Daniel respect this?)
☐ Does it use any forbidden words? (See §3)
☐ Does it sound like any anti-pattern? (See §2)
☐ Does it use exclamation marks? (Default: no)
☐ Does it use emojis in UI? (Always: no)
☐ Does it flatter the user? (Default: no)
☐ Does it ask permission unnecessarily? (Default: no)
☐ Could the same idea be said in fewer words? (If yes: cut)
☐ Could a more specific word replace a generic one? (If yes: replace)
☐ Does it sound like Yoodli, Duolingo, or a SaaS marketing page? (If yes: rewrite)
```

If any answer is wrong, rewrite. **No exceptions.**
