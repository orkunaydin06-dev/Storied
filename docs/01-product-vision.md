## 1. THE STRATEGIC THESIS

**The world has trained written thought. It has not trained spoken thought.**

For 500 years, schools, books, blogs, and articles have taught us to write better. Grammar checkers. Style guides. Editing software. Coursera. MasterClass.

But when it comes to **speaking** — the medium through which most of life's important moments happen — there is nothing comparable.

- The interview that decides your career: spoken.
- The pitch that decides your funding: spoken.
- The toast that defines your wedding: spoken.
- The story that connects you to your child: spoken.
- The argument that wins your team: spoken.

And yet, the tools to train spoken thought are either:
- **Too slow** (Toastmasters: weekly meetings, in-person, takes years)
- **Too generic** (ChatGPT: no structure, no discipline, no progress)
- **Too narrow** (Yoodli: counts filler words, misses meaning)
- **Too academic** (MasterClass: passive video, no practice)

### And there is a second, deeper problem.

**You already know the theory. You just never practiced it.**

You highlighted *Story* by Robert McKee. You watched the Aaron Sorkin MasterClass. You know about Aristotle's three acts. You've read about Pixar's story spine. You can explain Joseph Campbell's hero's journey at a dinner party.

But when the moment comes — the interview, the toast, the pitch — none of that knowledge transfers. Because **theory does not become craft until it is practiced.**

That gap — between **knowing** how stories work and **doing** stories well — is where smart people get stuck for years. They feel like they should be good at this. They've consumed all the right material. And yet, when they open their mouth, the magic isn't there.

**Storied exists to close this gap.**

A daily voice practice built on the methodologies you already know — Aristotle, Pixar, Campbell, Cicero — but designed for one thing only: making you actually do it. Every day. With your own stories. For thirty days.

This is not another book. Not another video course. Not another set of frameworks to add to the pile.

**This is where theory finally becomes craft.**

## 3. PERSONA VS AUDIENCE — A CRITICAL DISTINCTION

Before we describe who Storied is for, we need to separate two things that often get conflated.

### 3.0 — Persona vs Audience

**Persona** is a single, vivid character that guides every taste decision. When we ask "is this copy right?" or "does this feature belong?", we are really asking: *"Would this person respect this?"*

**Audience** is the broader market of people who will buy. It is plural, multi-role, and diverse.

We have one persona: **Daniel.** He keeps our standards high. He is the proxy for our taste.

We have many audience segments: **Founders. PMs. Consultants. Sales pros. Writers. Leaders. Therapists. Coaches. Lawyers. Doctors. Wedding speakers. Career switchers.** All of them share Daniel's DNA — they are thinkers who want to tell their own stories better — but they are not all Daniel.

**The rule:**
- Use Daniel to filter every product decision (copy, feature, vibe).
- Use the broader audience to design marketing reach (channels, segments, language).
- Never market to Daniel alone (too narrow). Never water down Daniel for the audience (loses taste).

The marketing message must feel personal to many people. The product must feel made for one.

**He cannot do what they do.** And he knows it.

### 4.2 — Daniel's Three Painful Moments

These are the specific situations where Daniel feels his storytelling failure most acutely:

**1. The All-Hands Presentation**

Last month he presented his product roadmap to 80 colleagues. He had great content. The deck was clean. The numbers were strong.

But halfway through, he saw it: people checking phones. The energy dropped. Someone yawned.

Afterwards, his manager said: *"Great content, Daniel. Maybe think about delivery next time."*

He went home and Googled "how to present better." Watched two TED Talk breakdowns. Felt overwhelmed. Closed the tabs.

**2. The Dinner Conversation**

He was at a dinner with his partner's colleagues. Someone asked: *"How did you two meet?"*

His partner started telling the story. It was beautiful. Specific. Funny. People leaned in.

Then she said: *"You tell the rest, Daniel."*

He stumbled. The story flattened. Someone politely smiled. The conversation moved on.

He spent the rest of the night replaying the moment in his head.

**3. The LinkedIn Post**

He started writing on LinkedIn 8 months ago. Career insights. Product management lessons. He has 1,200 followers.

His posts get 6 likes. Sometimes 12 on a good day.

He sees other PMs with smaller followings getting 500+ likes. He's read their posts. The ideas aren't better than his. **The way they're told** is better.

He's tried to copy their structure. It feels fake. He's tried to write like himself. It feels flat.

### 4.3 — Daniel's Failed Attempts at Solving It

**He tried Toastmasters.**

Went to two meetings. Found the people warm but the format painful. The "Toastmaster of the evening" wore a suit and spoke like a 1950s radio host. The icebreaker speech was 4-6 minutes — too long for someone testing the waters. Didn't return.

**He tried ChatGPT.**

Spent two evenings asking it to "help me become a better storyteller." Got generic advice ("use the rule of three!"). Asked it to grade his Slack messages. Got vague feedback. Lost interest within a week.

**He bought Yoodli.**

Recorded himself five times. Got reports about his pacing, filler words, eye contact (it asked for video, which felt invasive). The data was real but didn't tell him what he actually wanted to know: *was the story any good?* Cancelled after the trial.

**He bought StoryWorthy on Audible.**

Listened to 4 chapters. The content was good but it was a book, not a practice. He needed *reps*, not lectures.

**This is the core insight: Daniel has tried everything. He has consumed every framework. He has the knowledge. He just has never been forced to apply it, day after day, with feedback. That's the gap Storied fills.**

### 4.4 — Daniel's Aha Moment with Storied

He sees a LinkedIn post from a Turkish founder he follows (Orkun — that's you). The post says:

> *"For 12 weeks, I've recorded myself telling one story a day. Got AI feedback. Recorded again. The transformation has been audible. Try it: storied.app"*

There's a 30-second audio sample. Day 1 vs Day 30. The difference is undeniable.

Daniel clicks. The landing page is dark, quiet, serif. Says: *"The daily practice of being a storyteller."*

He reads the FAQ. He sees the price: $29. He sees the promise: 10 minutes a day, 30 days, built on Aristotle and Pixar and Hero's Journey.

He doesn't hesitate. He pays.

The next morning, his first practice begins.

## 6. THE COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE

### 6.1 — Direct Competitors (Speaking-Skill Apps)

#### Yoodli — The "Metric Counter"

**What they do:** Records video/audio, analyzes filler words, pacing, eye contact, sentiment. Reports back with metrics.

**What's good about them:** Real technology. Real data. Decent UX.

**What's broken:** They reward polish over substance. A user with zero filler words and perfect pacing can still tell a terrible story. Yoodli would give them a high score anyway.

**The Storied difference:** We don't measure *how* you spoke. We measure *what* you said. Storied evaluates Clarity, Structure, Depth, Impact — the actual quality of the story. Authenticity is a metric, not an enemy.

**Positioning line:** *"Yoodli counts your filler words. Storied teaches you to tell stories worth not interrupting."*

#### Speak / Loora / Lingvist — The "ESL Apps"

**What they do:** Help non-English speakers practice conversational English with AI.

**What's good:** Real product-market fit in a huge market.

**What's broken:** They're language teachers, not storytelling coaches. The bar is "did you say it correctly?" not "did you say it well?"

**The Storied difference:** We assume you can already speak the language. We're teaching the higher craft — the *what* and the *how*, not the words.

**Positioning line:** *"Speak teaches you English. Storied teaches you what to do with it."*

#### Final Round AI — The "Cheat Mode"

**What they do:** Real-time AI assistant that feeds you answers during live interviews.

**What's good:** Solves an acute pain (interview anxiety).

**What's broken:** It's a crutch, not a craft. You don't learn anything. The moment you uninstall, you're back to square one.

**The Storied difference:** We build the muscle. Forever. No crutch. No assistant whispering in your ear. Just you, getting better.

**Positioning line:** *"Final Round helps you cheat through one interview. Storied helps you become someone who doesn't need help."*

### 6.2 — Adjacent Competitors (General AI Tools)

#### ChatGPT / Claude / Gemini

**What they do:** Generic conversation. Can give feedback on speaking if you ask.

**What's good:** Free. Flexible. Smart.

**What's broken:** No structure. No discipline. No progress. No memory of what you did yesterday. The user has to design the entire practice. 99% of users don't.

**The Storied difference:** We are the **opinionated path**. We chose the curriculum. We chose the questions. We chose the methods. We chose the metrics. You just show up.

**Positioning line:** *"ChatGPT can help anyone do anything. Storied helps one person do one thing — become a storyteller."*

### 6.3 — Indirect Competitors (Traditional Methods)

#### Toastmasters

**What they do:** Weekly in-person speaking clubs since 1924.

**What's good:** Real community. Live feedback. Proven.

**What's broken:** Slow (weekly), location-dependent, formal culture, 12-week minimum to see structure benefits. The format feels dated to anyone under 40.

**The Storied difference:** Daily, private, modern. The same hours that would buy you 4 Toastmasters meetings buy you 30 Storied practices.

**Positioning line:** *"Toastmasters is dinner with a club. Storied is morning coffee with a mentor."*

#### MasterClass / Coursera Storytelling Courses

**What they do:** Passive video lectures from famous storytellers (Aaron Sorkin, Neil Gaiman).

**What's good:** Inspiring. Well-produced.

**What's broken:** No practice. You watch, you nod, you forget.

**The Storied difference:** We are not a course. We are a practice. Reps beat lectures, every time.

**Positioning line:** *"MasterClass shows you what great storytelling looks like. Storied makes you do it."*

#### Books (Storyworthy, Story, StoryBrand, Sapiens)

**What they do:** Teach storytelling theory.

**What's good:** Deep. Cheap. Available.

**What's broken:** Reading about practice is not practice. 95% of buyers don't finish the book.

**The Storied difference:** We compress the wisdom of these books into 30 daily 10-minute practices. The user gets the theory **embedded in the doing**.

### 7.6 — WHY THE PROGRESSION MATTERS

This is Storied's most defensible competitive advantage. It deserves its own section on the landing page **and** its own dedicated `/methodology` page. Antigravity must build both.

**The headline for this section:**

> **Not a course. Not a list of tips. A craft progression — five weeks, five masters, one voice.**

**The narrative:**

Storied is not 30 random questions. It is a **deliberate progression** — five weeks, five methodologies, designed in a specific order that compounds.

**Why Aristotle first?**
Because every story rests on structure and conflict. Without these foundations, no later technique can stick. Week 1 builds the skeleton. You learn beginning-middle-end, anagnorisis (recognition), peripeteia (the turn). Two thousand three hundred years of foundation.

**Why Pixar second?**
Because once you understand classical structure, you can see how Pixar made it modern. The Story Spine — "Once upon a time… Every day… One day… Because of that…" — is Aristotle, distilled into six beats anyone can use today. Week 2 gives you a tool you can apply in any context: emails, pitches, calls, dinners.

**Why Joseph Campbell third?**
Because by week three, you're ready to turn the lens inward. Your own life is the material. The Hero's Journey shows you how to find the mythic shape inside your own experiences — the call, the refusal, the mentor, the threshold, the ordeal, the return. This is where your stories become **yours**, not formula.

**Why Cicero fourth?**
Because by week four, you have stories — and you're ready to learn how to make them land. Rhetoric is the art of being heard. Cicero perfected it 2,000 years ago. *Inventio* (find the argument), *dispositio* (arrange it), *elocutio* (style it), *movere* (move the audience). The methods that built Western persuasion.

**Why Synthesis fifth?**
Because in the final week, you stop following anyone else's framework. You take what you've learned and find **your own voice**. This is what separates a storyteller from a story-teller-by-formula. The greatest writers and speakers throughout history didn't follow rules — they internalized them, then transcended them. Week 5 is yours.

**The closing thought:**

This is not an arbitrary syllabus. It is a craft progression — the same one used in the world's best writing programs, distilled into thirty days of voice practice. Each week is a layer. Each layer compounds. By Day 30, you don't sound like a Pixar imitator or a Cicero pastiche. You sound like **you, but clearer**.

## 9. THE FAQ (FOR LANDING PAGE)

These eight questions cover 90% of pre-purchase doubts. Each answer is written in Storied's voice — direct, honest, no hedging.

**Q: Why $29? Is it too cheap to be serious?**

It's $29 because we're building. The first 50 customers get founding pricing. After that, it's $39. After 200 customers, $49. The product is the same. The price is just the early access tax.

Because 30 practices is 30 practices. We sell you a journey, not a treadmill. When you finish, you can come back for the next category (coming soon). But we won't auto-charge you for a service you stopped using.

**Q: What if I miss days?**

Nothing happens. Storied counts practices, not calendar days. You can do all 30 in a month, or take six months. The work waits for you. We won't send you guilt emails.

**Q: Do I need to be a native English speaker?**

You need to be **functional** in English. Storied isn't a language app — it teaches you what to do with the words you already have. If you can hold a normal conversation in English, you'll get value here. If you can't, learn the language first, then come back.

**Q: Will I be a great storyteller after 30 days?**

You'll be a much better one. Whether that's "great" depends on where you started. What we can promise: you'll **hear** the difference between Day 1 and Day 30. If you don't, we'll refund you. (See refund policy below.)

**Q: What does the AI feedback actually say?**

It's specific, honest, and rooted in the day's method. Example: "You started in the middle. Aristotle would tell you we need to feel the ordinariness before we feel the change. Try opening with one concrete detail — a smell, a time, a single word." No vague encouragement. No flattery.

**Q: Is my audio private?**

Yes, absolutely. Your recordings live in a private storage bucket only you can access. We never share them, never use them to train AI models, never analyze them for marketing. You can delete your entire archive with one click. (See full privacy policy.)

**Q: Refund policy?**

If you don't hear meaningful improvement between Day 1 and Day 30, we refund you. No questions. We trust the practice. If it doesn't work for you, you shouldn't pay for it.

### 12.1 — Months 1-3: Storytelling Only, Polish to Perfection

Single category. Single price. Single message.

Goal: 50 paying customers. Real testimonials. Real product-market validation.

What we **do**:

### 12.2 — Months 4-6: Quietly Add the Second Category

After Phase 1 validates, we add **one more category**. The candidates:

**Option A: Pitching**
- Natural extension of storytelling
- Same persona (Daniel, but as founder/PM pitching ideas)
- Methodologies: YC pitch format, Sequoia deck, Pixar pitch
- Easier to market (clearer ROI)

**Option B: Negotiation**
- Adjacent skill, different mental muscle
- Methodologies: Chris Voss (adapted), Harvard Method, Cicero (we already have him)
- Higher willingness-to-pay (people see ROI in dollars)
- Riskier (more polarizing market)

**Recommendation:** Pitching. It compounds with storytelling (same skill, applied) rather than competing.

Once two categories are live, introduce a bundle:

**Storied Foundation Pack: $79** (vs $58 separate)
- Storytelling 30-day practice
- Pitching 30-day practice

### 12.4 — Months 10-12: Third Category + Mobile Native

Third category decision (likely Negotiation or Public Speaking — TBD based on user feedback).

Mobile native app (React Native, shared codebase with web). Only built if mobile usage is >60% of total usage by month 9.

By month 18, Storied could be:

- 4-5 categories (Storytelling, Pitching, Negotiation, Public Speaking, Critical Thinking)
